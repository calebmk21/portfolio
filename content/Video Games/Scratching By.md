## Project Overview
At the start of my junior year at UCLA, some friends and I decided to throw our hat into the ring for Ludum Dare 51. We had assembled a team of 7 that would be a semi-regular game jam group. With a theme of “Every 10 Seconds” we thought about our beloved CoolMathGames, specifically the Papa’s games (I’m most partial to Papa’s Freezeria myself) and got to work. We settled on a cat cafe that sells boba, after we all collectively brainstormed over, well, boba. Eventually, the cats will order pastries and coffee, but personally I’ve never made it that far, as cats spawn into the queue every 10 seconds. It’s intense, and I don’t think I’ve ever seen anybody actually endure the full 10 minutes of the game (yes, this is a challenge).

## My Role
I was one of the UI/UX designers and implementers, and by this point I was already somewhat familiar with Unity’s UI systems. Every one of us also took part in the discussion for game design, and I specifically took inspiration from Papa’s Freezeria when designing the brainstorming the gameplay loop. As a fun aside, the pop sound effect you hear in the menus is actually voiced by me. Another fun challenge is that most of the UI button text (as well as the credits page) was hand written by me. Also, since this was a 72 hour game jam, I cooked dinner for everybody that day. It was an unforgettable experience, and easily one of my favorite weekends at UCLA.
