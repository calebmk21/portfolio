## Project Overview
This game was a passion project that I co-led during ACM Studio’s Students Run Studios program in 2023. I played Metroid Dread the summer of 2022 and decided this would be a fun style of game to make. Despite realizing I love the Metroidvania genre, I still have not completed Hollow Knight, but that’s for another time. Nonetheless, I love the storytelling from Metroid Dread and Hollow Knight (from what I have played), and I love using the ambient environment to tell a story.

### Link to Game
https://wozard.itch.io/requiem-of-the-frogs

### Inspiration
This specific idea that I had came after being inspired by a few things, namely the loss of my grandmother in 2021 and the song 言って (romanized as "Itte") by Yorushika (ヨルシカ), which I was listening to nonstop during the summer of 2022. I was very inspired by the idea of making a heartfelt story about learning to accept loss, and it was a deeply personal one since this was rooted in my own experiences.

### Conception
I remember the day I pitched the idea to my friend Faustine, who then agreed to be my co-lead for the project. I was very excited because I had wanted to lead a team for my club’s program, and being able to do it with one of my best friends was a dream. I definitely think I sold her on the idea because the game was centered around frogs. Frogs are amazing. We bounced ideas back and forth, and we decided to draw on my own Korean heritage and have our protagonist be a Korean treasure hunter. We settled on Daehyun Cho / 조 대현 as the name of our protagonist, and Sooyeon Cho / 조 수연 as the name of his twin sister. I wanted Daehyun to go through immense loss where he feels as though he is to blame. One of our earliest stretch goals (which was scrapped due to the immense scale) was to allow the player to choose their protagonist and then the other one will die, resulting in two different stories.

### Story Outline
I had a very solid idea for how I wanted Daehyun’s character arc to play out, inspired by the five stages of grief. It was a simple outline, but I had a very grand plan for the nuanced interactions, with Frog Spirits to guide Daehyun. Of course, due to time and work constraints, we didn’t get to implement most of the story, however my idea document is linked [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1k2JxNwdaV0AD75RMPZFDYa79X5Dvcr6VGlubRMcyrbs/edit?usp=sharing)
<br>
Although I conceived some of these ideas prior to going in (after all, I needed a pitch), I cannot emphasize the work of my team — we bounced ideas off of each other and ultimately settled on such a nice story that I wish could have been implemented. We worked to craft the narrative so much that I might revisit this someday to write this as a story, but that’s for another time.

### Leading a Team
This was the first time I had ever led a team, and I really loved it. I can say with certainty that I could not have done this without my wonderful co-lead, Faustine. She and I learned together and complemented each other very nicely, or at least I’d like to think so. While the final product wasn’t nearly as much as I had planned, it was an experience I would never trade in for the world.

Sometimes I look back on the team and wonder if I made the right choices. I chose to be a lenient lead, something that Faustine and I incidentally both decided to do. I wanted to take a more sympathetic approach. After all, we’re just students. We have other responsibilities to attend to like our own lives and classes, not to mention we’re not getting paid. Finding a balance in being firm yet sympathetic is something that I’m still learning. Above anything else, I wanted to make sure they enjoyed the process and learned a lot. I can only hope that I did right by them.
